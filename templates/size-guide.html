How to Measure

Getting the right measurements is key to finding your perfect fit. Follow our simple measuring guide:

Foot Measurement

1
Prepare

Stand on a flat surface with your heel against a wall

2
Measure Length

Measure from heel to longest toe (usually the big toe)

3
Measure Width

Measure the widest part of your foot

4
Both Feet

Measure both feet and use the larger measurement

Body Measurement

1
Chest

Measure around the fullest part of your chest

2
Waist

Measure around your natural waistline

3
Hips

Measure around the fullest part of your hips

4
Inseam

Measure from crotch to desired pant length

Measuring Tips

  • Measure in the afternoon when feet are slightly swollen
  • Wear the type of socks you'll wear with the shoes
  • Use a flexible measuring tape for body measurements
  • Keep the tape snug but not tight
  • Measure over thin clothing for accurate body measurements

Regional Fit Notes

European Sizing

Generally runs smaller than US sizes. Consider sizing up for a comfortable fit.

US Sizing

Standard fit with room for layering. True to size for most body types.

Asian Sizing

Typically runs 1-2 sizes smaller than Western sizing.

Fit Tips

Athletic Footwear

  • Leave 1/2 inch (1.3cm) space between longest toe and shoe end
  • Consider width for comfort during activity
  • Try shoes in the afternoon when feet are slightly swollen
  • Wear appropriate socks when trying on

Casual Footwear

  • Should fit snugly but not tightly
  • Heel should not slip when walking
  • Consider the type of socks you'll wear
  • Leather shoes may stretch slightly over time

Tops & Shirts

  • Shoulder seams should sit at your shoulder edge
  • Chest should have 2-3 inches of room for movement
  • Sleeve length should reach wrist bone
  • Length should cover waistband when arms raised

Bottoms & Pants

  • Waist should fit comfortably without a belt
  • Inseam length should break at shoe top
  • Hips should have room for movement
  • Consider rise (low, mid, high) preference

Product-Specific Fit Information

Running Shoes

Size up 1/2 size from regular shoes for toe room during runs

Basketball Shoes

True to size with ankle support consideration

Hiking Boots

Size up for thick socks and foot swelling on trails

Performance Wear

Compression fit - consider size down for snug fit

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I'm between sizes?

If you're between sizes, we recommend sizing up for comfort, especially for footwear.

Do sizes vary between brands?

Yes, sizing can vary between brands. Always check the specific product's size chart.

How do I know if shoes fit properly?

Your toes should have about 1/2 inch of space, and your heel shouldn't slip when walking.

Can I exchange for a different size?

Yes, we offer free size exchanges within 30 days of delivery.
